Bios

Hamed Mohsenian-Rad, Ph.D., IEEE Fellow, is a Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering and a Bourns Family Faculty Fellow at UC Riverside, USA. His research is on developing data-driven and modelbased techniques for monitoring, control, and optimization in power systems. He has received the NSF CAREER Award, a Best Paper Award from the IEEE PES General Meeting, a Best Paper Award from the IEEE Conference on Smart Grid Communications, and the Technical Achievement Award from the IEEE Communications Society. He is the author of Smart Grid Sensors: Principles and Applications, Cambridge University Press, 2022, a textbook on smart grid sensors and data-driven applications. He has served as the Lead PI for over $10 million smart grid research projects from the DoE, CEC, and NSF. He is currently an Associate Editor of the IEEE Transactions on Power Systems. He received his Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of British Columbia, Vancouver, Canada in 2008.

Abstract

Synchro-waveform data (i.e., time-synchronized waveform measurements) is a new concept in power systems data analytics has promising applications in power systems situational awareness. In this talk, we first make comparisons, based on real-world data, between the waveform measurements from Waveform Measurement Units (WMUs) and the phasor measurements from PMUs (and Harmonic PMUs). Next, we study different real-world examples, from recent WMU pilot installations, to showcase how synchrowaveform data can capture the same physical phenomenon and the most inconspicuous disturbances in the power system, as seen by the WMUs at different locations. Data analytics methods will be discussed next, to translate waveform data into actionable information for practical use cases. Feature extractions, in timedomain and in graphical forms, as well as hybrid data-driven and model-based methods, will be discussed, with focus on the analysis of sub-cycle and transient events. Emerging applications of waveforms and synchro-waveforms will be presented, in asset monitoring, incipient fault detection and identification, operation of inverter-based resources, as well as the analysis of power system dynamics.
