Bios

Dr. Saikat Chakrabarti is working as Professor and Emera Research Chair in Smart Grid Technologies and the Director of the Emera and NB Power Research Centre for Smart Grid Technologies, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of New Brunswick (UNB). Prior to joining UNB, he was working as a Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, India. His research interests are in the areas of power system state estimation, power system dynamics and stability, smart grid, and microgrid. He is a Fellow of the Indian National Academy of Engineering and a Fellow of the Institution of Engineers (India).

Abstract

Modern power systems are experiencing unprecedented variability in generation, load, and operating conditions due to several reasons including large penetration of intermittent renewable energy sources, growing consumer participation, and changing market scenario. Data from measurement and sensing devices and other sources are, therefore, playing increasingly important role in monitoring, control, and protection of the power system. Two of the recent technologies: synchronized measurement technology at the generation and transmission level, and advanced metering infrastructure at the low voltage distribution level, are revolutionizing the energy management centre applications. The talk will highlight the prominent features of these two technologies, in addition to the traditional supervisory control and data acquisition system. Various other sources that contribute useful data for power system will also be discussed. Some of the challenges in handling and combining these big data sources will also be mentioned in the talk.
