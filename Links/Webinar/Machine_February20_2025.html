Bios

Dr. Hongyu Wu is a Lucas-Rathbone Professor and University Outstanding Scholar in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Kansas State University. His research focuses on cybersecurity, AI-driven analytics for power systems, and grid resilience. He is an NSF CAREER Awardee and has led multiple NSF-, DOE-, and DoD-funded projects. Dr. Wu serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid and IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics.

Abstract

As machine learning (ML) becomes a critical tool in power system operations, adversaries are developing advanced attack strategies to evade ML-based anomaly detection. This tutorial explores both ML-driven defenses and stealthy attack techniques, with a focus on false data injection (FDI) attacks that manipulate power system state estimation. Key topics include: 1. Machine Learning for Cybersecurity in Power Systems – How ML-based anomaly detection methods identify cyberattacks and their limitations against adversarial threats. 2. Low-Rank and Tensor-Completion-Based Stealthy FDI Attacks – How attackers can use low-rank approximation and tensor completion to modify measurements while preserving statistical consistency, making them difficult for ML models to detect. 3. Defense Strategies: Machine Learning and Moving Target Defense – Techniques such as reactance perturbation strategies (RPS) and adaptive ML models that actively counter stealthy cyberattacks. By bridging big data analytics, cybersecurity, and power system operation, this tutorial will provide insights into AI-driven attack detection, vulnerabilities in existing defenses, and emerging trends in cybersecurity for the smart grid.
